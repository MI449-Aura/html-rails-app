Eating Disorders

Eating disorders are serious but treatable mental health conditions characterized by unhealthy relationships with food, eating, and body image. They affect people of all ages, genders, and backgrounds, and are never a choice or a reflection of character. If you or someone you know is struggling, please reach out to a healthcare professional or contact the National Alliance for Eating Disorders helpline for support.

Eating Disorders

Sometimes, people can develop confusing thoughts and feelings about food and their bodies that make it hard for them to eat in a healthy way. This is called an eating disorder, and it's not something they choose to think. Just like getting sick or breaking a bone, eating disorders are a health problem. Some people might not eat enough while others eat too much then feel bad afterwards. Good news though, eating disorders can be treated with the help from doctors, counselors, and a loving support system. Remember it is always ok to talk to a trusted grown up if you or someone you love is struggling with these thoughts. You are never alone and you are beautiful.
